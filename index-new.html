HATHAWAY


CHRISTINE KELLER


CREATIVE DIRECTOR



Hathaway Christine Keller, creative director of Karine, has had a phenomenal style trajectory. The British-born designer began her career just weeks out of school, moving to New York at just 21 years-old to take a job working in womenswear at Calvin Klein. Four years later she moved on to a post at Ralph Lauren, where she worked directly under the eponymous designer for the launch of the brand’s Purple Label menswear collection - a position that gave her the deep understanding and appreciation of tailoring that influences her ‘borrowed-from-the-boys’ aesthetic seen at Karine today.
When Tom Ford was looking for a key member of his design team to work with Francisco Costa and Christopher Bailey at Gucci’s design studio in London in 2000, he looked to Keller. She remained there until Ford’s departure in 2004 and the following year was appointed creative director of Pringle of Scotland. Over the next six years, she injected a refreshed and modern context into the heritage knitwear label, catapulting it into the international fashion spotlight.
Moving to Paris with her young family, Keller joined Karine in June 2011, immersing herself in the French city. “The energy is different in Paris. It’s classic and precise and there’s a tension between modernity and heritage here which affects my approach".

For Keller, Karine is unique. “Karine speaks to me on so many levels,” she says. “Firstly because it’s feminine. I love the free-spirited, effortless attitude of the Karine girl. It’s about those fabulous pieces you wear so many different ways and the spirit of confidence you have when you wear them. It’s real.”

Keller has defined her approach at Karine with collections that fuse the traditional femininity of the Maison's design heritage with masculine influences. Her work has a graphic sensibility articulated through clean lines, refined silhouettes and a cool and modern attitude. Unexpected colour combinations and unique palettes have become a signature and she has introduced bold jewellery as a strong statement to her looks. Her collections continue to excite and inspire a growing audience of fashion influencers and #KarineGIRLS.
